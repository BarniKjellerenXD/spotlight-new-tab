# Privacy Policy for Spotlight New Tab

Last updated: June 20, 2026

## Overview
**Spotlight New Tab** is a Chromium extension that replaces your browser's new tab page with a spotlight-style search interface. This privacy policy explains what data the extension accesses, how it is used, and what data (if any) is shared.

## Data Collection
**This extension does NOT collect, store, or transmit any personal data.** All operations are performed locally within your browser.

## Permissions Explained

### Bookmarks, History, Top Sites, Tabs
These permissions are used **solely to power the search functionality**:
- **bookmarks** — To search your bookmarks when you type in the search bar
- **history** — To search your browsing history for matching pages
- **topSites** — To show your most-visited websites as quick links on the new tab page
- **tabs** — To find and switch to already-open tabs matching your search query

No bookmark, history, or tab data is ever sent to any remote server. All searching happens locally on your device.

### Storage
Used **only** to save your settings preferences (which search sources are enabled, theme toggle, etc.) locally via `chrome.storage.local`. This data never leaves your browser.

### Search
Used to open a web search in your browser when you activate the "Search the web" result. This simply triggers your default search engine — no search terms are transmitted by the extension itself.

## Data Sharing
**We do not share, sell, or transmit any data.** There are no analytics, tracking scripts, or remote servers involved. The extension has no network access other than what you explicitly trigger by clicking a search result or web link.

## Third-Party Services
When you click a search result or web link, your browser navigates to that URL as you would expect. The extension itself does not communicate with any third-party services.
